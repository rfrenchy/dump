Inanimate Objects

Inanimate objects move predictably under known forces. For example, an object falls with an acceleration of ~9.8m p/s

  • 1/8 second - 0.08m - 3 frames
  • 1/4 second - 0.3m - 6 frames
  • 1/2 second - 1.2m - 12 frames
  • 1 second - 4.9m - 24 frames

Slow Actions

The closers the animation drawings are to each, the slower the movement appears.

Slower movement are harder to animate effectively due to the nature of the animation medium. Productions tend to use live action for this instead.

Ω Why difficult? Hard for animator to get the level of precision require for every small inch of movement in each muscle

Fast Actions

Easier to animate compared to slow actions. Strength of the animation medium as you can exaggerate and add pace and energy in drawings for effectively than in real life actions

Audience must be able to read whats happening through the fast sequence. Make as readable as neccesary.
